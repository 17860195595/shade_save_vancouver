ShadeSafe Vancouver

Live heat-stress awareness for public spaces — map-first, mobile-first, community-powered.

How risk scores work

ShadeSafe blends temperature, UV index, tree and canopy shade (as a 0–100% “shade score” per location), and solar elevation from SunCalc for Vancouver. Map pins are Vancouver parks from the City’s parks open dataset (not hand-picked). As the sun climbs, exposed plazas and beaches heat up faster than well-treed parks. The time slider lets you preview how risk shifts through the day using the same model.

Community reporting

  • Too hot — pins a hot spot on the heatmap for others.
  • Great shade — highlights relief; the heatmap can cool slightly at that pin.
  • Needs shade structure — flags where infrastructure like sails or plantings would help most.

Heat layer & colours

The heatmap is separate from the green park rings (those only mark park locations from open data). Small green dots are street trees loaded for your current map view.

Each park pin feeds the heat layer with an intensity based on how little shade the model estimates there (lower shade score → more “sun exposure”) and how high the sun is at the hour you set on the slider. Slider times are Vancouver local (America/Vancouver), not your device time zone. At night the sun factor is near zero, so the layer should read cooler unless community “too hot” pins add heat.

Colours run from green (more shade / lower intensity) through orange to red. Lighter red sits in the mid-high range of that scale; darker red is the strongest blend. Nearby points overlap and blur, so dense areas can look deeper red than a single isolated pin.

Community reports tweak the layer: “Too hot” adds a strong warm bump at that spot; “Great shade” nudges it cooler there.
